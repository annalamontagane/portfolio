The abroadDiaries

Team

Anna Lamontagne

My Roles

Videographer & Editor

Duration

Ongoing, 3 years

Overview

The abroadDiaries is my ongoing travel vlog series, a personal project I’ve been building over the past three years. It started as a way to capture moments for my future self, something closer to a visual journal than a traditional vlog.

Each video focuses less on documenting where I went and more on capturing how it felt to be there. Through editing, sound, and pacing, I try to recreate a sense of atmosphere that makes the experience feel more immersive and memorable.

Background

Over time, the project evolved into something more intentional. Instead of just recording footage, I began thinking more carefully about how pacing, sound, and sequencing could shape how a place is remembered.

Process

My editing process is music-first. I choose a track that reflects the feeling of a place, and then build the entire sequence around it. The pacing, cuts, and visual rhythm are all shaped by that initial decision. I've come to beleive that sound deisgn is perhaps the most impactful aspect of storytelling.

I also place a strong emphasis on sound. I often layer in ambient audio or remove music entirely in certain moments so that the natural sounds of a place can come through. These quieter sections tend to ground the video and make the experience feel more real.

Most of the footage is captured spontaneously. Rather than planning shots in advance, I respond to what’s happening in the moment, which makes the editing process more about discovering structure afterward than following a predefined plan.

Over time, my editing style has shifted. Earlier videos were more fast-paced, influenced by shorter form content and quicker cuts. More recently, I’ve started to slow things down, allowing certain moments to linger longer. I now try to balance faster sequences with more drawn-out shots to better capture the overall atmosphere.

Solution

The result is a series of videos that function as both documentation and interpretation. Each piece combines music, ambient sound, and visual sequencing to create something that feels closer to a memory than a traditional vlog.

The structure is intentionally loose, allowing each video to adapt to the tone of the location rather than forcing a consistent format across all episodes.

Results

Over the past three years, the project has grown into a consistent body of work that reflects both different places and how my approach to storytelling has evolved.

It has also become a personal archive, allowing me to revisit experiences in a way that feels more immediate and complete than photos or written notes alone.

Learnings

This project has shaped how I think about storytelling, especially the role of pacing and sound. Small decisions, like when to cut or when to leave space, have a significant impact on how something feels.

It also taught me to be more comfortable with slower moments. Letting a shot linger, rather than constantly moving forward, often creates a stronger sense of presence.

More than anything, it reinforced that sometimes the goal isn’t to explain an experience, but to recreate it.
