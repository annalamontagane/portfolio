Long Form Video Editing

Team

Anna Lamontagne, Eva Meloche

My Roles

Assistant Video Editor

Duration

Ongoing freelance collaboration

Overview

I support Eva Meloche editing long-form video content for her lifestyle and travel YouTube channel. Shaping footage into stories that feel natural, cohesive, and aligned with her voice. My role goes beyond assembling clips, it’s about pacing, emotional rhythm, and translating someone else’s creative perspective into a finished piece.

Background

Eva’s content has a strong and recognizable tone, built around a balance of spontaneity and intention. The role of the editor is not just to structure footage, but to preserve that tone while shaping it into something viewers can follow and stay engaged with.

This creates a unique challenge: the goal is not to impose a new style, but to work within an existing creative language and make decisions that feel consistent with it.

Process

My process centers around understanding and aligning with Eva’s creative vision. Rather than approaching each video with a fixed structure, I focus on interpreting tone, pacing, and emotional flow, and making decisions that feel true to her style.

This involves shaping rhythm through timing, sequencing, and restraint. I think carefully about when to let a moment breathe and when to move quickly, using pacing to support how the video feels rather than just how it progresses.

Due to the nature of the work, I focus on maintaining a flexible and reliable workflow that allows for quick iteration while supporting creative exploration.

Solution

The result is a story-first editing approach that prioritizes alignment over imposition. Rather than defining the style, my role is to reinforce it — making decisions that support a consistent tone while improving clarity, pacing, and engagement.

Each video is shaped to feel effortless and natural, while still being carefully constructed beneath the surface.

Results

The collaboration has led to more cohesive and consistent videos, with stronger narrative clarity and a more defined editorial rhythm. By focusing on alignment, each piece of content reinforces Eva’s overall brand and creative identity.

The ongoing nature of the work has also allowed for a more refined and repeatable process, where each iteration builds on a deeper understanding of tone and pacing.

Learnings

This work taught me how to design within someone else’s creative vision rather than my own. It pushed me to become more attentive to nuance, understanding not just what works, but what feels right within a specific voice.

I also learned that strong collaboration is less about control and more about alignment. The best edits are not the ones that stand out, but the ones that feel seamless within the creator’s world.

More than anything, this experience reinforced that “natural” is rarely accidental. It is the result of careful, often invisible decisions.
